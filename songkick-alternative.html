What happened

Why people are leaving Songkick in 2026

In late 2025, Songkick was acquired by Suno — the AI music generation company. This means Suno is now the data controller for your Songkick account: your concert attendance history, your followed artists, your location data, your engagement signals.

For many users, that's the line. Concert history feels personal — it's a record of your life as a music fan, not an asset to power generative AI. The communities on Reddit have been vocal: account deletions spiked immediately after the news broke.

"My concert history is being turned into training data for AI music systems I never agreed to support."

— Sentiment across r/Concerts and r/indieheads, 2025

Whether or not that's exactly what Suno intends, the perception has shifted. Songkick is no longer a neutral utility. And you have GDPR rights — including the right to request erasure and to object to changes in how your data is processed.

Songkick alternatives — FAQ

What is the best Songkick alternative in 2026?

For concert discovery and show alerts, Bandsintown is currently the closest like-for-like replacement. For fans who care about owning their concert history permanently, Afterglow (early access) is built specifically around that — GPS-verified check-ins, a permanent passport, and no AI company behind it. JamBase is strong for US indie and festival-heavy tastes.

Why are people leaving Songkick?

Songkick was acquired by Suno — an AI music generation company — in late 2025. Suno is now the data controller for all Songkick accounts: concert attendance history, followed artists, location data, engagement signals. Many users interpret this as their personal fan data being repurposed for AI systems they never consented to support, and have deleted their accounts as a result.

Can I export or delete my Songkick data?

Yes. Under GDPR you have the right to request a full export of your data, the right to erasure (the right to be forgotten), and the right to object to your data being processed for new purposes — such as AI training. Contact Songkick's support or data protection officer directly to exercise these rights.

Does Afterglow replace Songkick?

Afterglow is built around a different core idea: a permanent, GPS-verified concert passport rather than a discovery feed. You can import your past show history from day one, so you don't start from scratch. It's currently in invite-only early access, rolling out city by city — you can request access via the waitlist below.

Is Bandsintown a good replacement for Songkick?

For the core discovery use case — following artists and getting show alerts — yes, Bandsintown is the strongest direct replacement available today. It has a large global database and a solid mobile app. Its weaknesses are that it has no meaningful concert history tracking, and social features are limited compared to what fans want.
